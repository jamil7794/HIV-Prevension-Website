About HIV

HIV stands for human immunodeficiency virus. HIV attacks and destroys the infection-fighting
CD4 cells that work in the immune system. The loss of CD4 cells makes it difficult for the body
to fight infections and diseases. AIDS stands for acquired immunodeficiency syndrome and
is the most advanced stage of HIV infection with no cure.

How can I get it?

HIV can be passed through the following body fluids:

  • Blood
  • Semen/pre-seminal fluid
  • Vaginal fluid
  • Breast Milk

    • HIV can also be passed through the following methods:

  • Sharing needles (either through drug use, unsafe tattooing, or piercing)
  • Childbirth
  • Blood Transfusions

    Resources on prevention
  • PrEP - Stands for pre-exposure prophylaxis. It is a pill that is taken daily to help prevent HIV. PrEP can lower the chances of contracting HIV by more than 90%.
  • Condoms - Use a condom everytime during sex to prevent transmission of HIV or other STDs.
  • Get tested for HIV and other STDs to be knowledgeable of your status.
  • Know your partner's status - Be aware of whether your partner has or might have HIV or STDs in order to protect yourself.
